About University

Naypyitaw State Polytechnic University is one of the best universities in Myanmar. Naypyitaw State Polytechnic University is a computer and technology university located in Mingalar Dipa quarter, Pobba Thiri Township, Naypyitaw Council Area. It is a university under the Department of Advanced Science and Technology, Ministry of Science and Technology.

    • Admission / Permission

    Naypitaw State Polytechnic University is based on the results of the Myanmar University Entrance Examination (also known as the Matriculation Examination). Students who have passed this exam with a minimum total score in subjects like English, Mathematics, Chemistry, and Physics are eligible to apply. The specific required marks can vary each academic year and are determined by the university and the government

    History

    Chairman of the State Administration Council Prime Minister Senior General Min Aung Hlaing inaugurated Naypyitaw Technological University on November 12th , 2022, with aims in providing high quality human resources which is vital for the development of the Nation, to support reginal development and social-economic well-being of the citizens, and 6 new specialized engineering subjects in 2022-2023 academic year as well as specialization subject on agricultural engineering is also introduced in 2023-2024 academic year.On November 21st of 2024, Chairman of the State Administration Council Prime Minister General Min Aung Hlaing relocated and expanded Naypyitaw Technological University to Naypyitaw State Polytechnic University (NSPU) in Pobba Thiri Township, Mingalar Dipa quarter at the junction of Yaza Thingaha Road and Thiri Mandaing Road, with the university premise area of 68 acres, in accordance with the 100th Anniversary of engineering education.The university comprises 16 academic departments, including seven engineering majors, four computer majors, and five supporting departments. It offers seven engineering degrees and two computer science degrees, admitting 270 engineering students and 120 computer science students annually. The programs are designed to integrate theoretical knowledge with practical applications, preparing students for real-world challenges.
