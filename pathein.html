About University

Technological University (Pathein) is situated at the Apin-ne-se village which is in Pathein District, the Ayeyawady Division, Myanmar. It was opened on 27 December 1999 as Government Technological College (G.T.C) and became Technological University (Pathein) on 20 January 2007. The university is run by Ministry of Education (Myanmar).

    • Admission / Permission

    Admission to Technological University (Pathein) is based on the results of the Myanmar University Entrance Examination (also known as the Matriculation Examination). Students who have passed this exam with a minimum total score in subjects like English, Mathematics, Chemistry, and Physics are eligible to apply. The specific required marks can vary each academic year and are determined by the university and the government

    History

    1999: The institution was founded on December 27 as the Government Technological College (GTC) in Pathein. It initially operated out of the former Cooperative Training School in Lun-Kan Ward and offered a four-year Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech) degree..
    2002: The college relocated to a new campus, situated in the Twenty Plants Village area of Kan-gyi-daung Township, near the Pathein-Kyan-Khin Railway. The 27.45-acre campus was completed and opened on August 1, 2002..
    2007: The Government Technological College was officially upgraded to the level of Pathein Technological University on January 20.
    After 2007: With its new university status, the institution began offering a broader range of engineering degrees, awarding Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) degrees in fields such as Civil, Electronic, Electrical Power, Mechanical, and Information Technology.
